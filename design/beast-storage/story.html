Why retain the 2 TB drive?

PROPOSED
OverviewLifecycle flowOperator dashboardDecision story

01 / FACTS

The installed drive is healthy and spacious.

APP has about 1.8 TiB free. SMART reports 44 °C, 1% lifetime used, 100% spare, and no media errors.

MEASURED 2026-07-11

02 / DECISION

Keep it.

A smaller 512 GB drive saves no meaningful weight or power, drops rated endurance from 600 TBW to 150 TBW, and limits sensor capture.

PROPOSED

03 / BOUNDARY

Make data portable, not partitions complex.

`/data/beast` is the only consumer contract. It can later be mounted separately; there is no immediate benefit in reflash, repartition, quota, Docker migration, or dual-root complexity.

PROPOSED

04 / EXIT CRITERIA

Prove the physical loop before continuous capture.

  • Observe the actual ROS topic graph.
  • Replay black-box and full-sensor missions.
  • Measure rate, CPU, retention, and free-space guards.
  • Only then enable black box.
